Unexploded Munition Sites in NYC

By Kalen Goodluck
@kalengoodluck

Thousands of acres of land formerly used by the US military for firing ranges and weapon testing are now left with unexploded munitions and contaminated from weapons disposal. These military bases and their respective abandoned firing ranges are zones of limited access and perception to civilians, yet they are scattered near communities.

In 2001, Representative Bluemenauer claimed unexploded ordnance had killed 65 people. Since then, the Environmental Protection Agency and Department of Defense compiled a database that identifies where UXO (unexploded ordnances) sites are.

While not all of these sites pose immediate danger, these sites are in the process of cleanup and disarmament of possible unexploded munitions.
